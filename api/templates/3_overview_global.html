Global Fatalities by Region

By year: 2018 - 2022

When we analyse the total fatalities its apparent that the overall data remains consistent with certain regions such as Western Africa and Northern America having the most fatalities.

As we drill down further into this data, 2020 surprisingly has the second highest total of fatalities, much to our surprise since we initially thought the pandemic and global lockdowns would have more of a significant impact to the total of fatalities.

Across all years Eastern Asia appears to have the lowest total of fatalities, this may be due to regulations around the data they choose to publish and share with the world.

Southern Africa steadily has the lowest number of fatalities in comparison to other parts of Africa, particularly Western Africa which has the highest amount each year.

Europe also fell into one of the lowest regions for fatalities until 2022, with Ukraine accounting for most of these fatalities which depicts how impactful the Russian invasion has been.


Data Source: ACLED - Anti-Civilian Violence Dataset (10 June 2022)
